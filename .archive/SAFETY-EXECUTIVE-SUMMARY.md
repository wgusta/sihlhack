## Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive safety analysis of the Sihlhack concept and event. The analysis follows a rigorous truth-seeking methodology to identify all safety risks, regulatory gaps, and provide specific recommendations.

## Critical Recommendations

### Must Implement Before Pilot Event

1. **Secure Event Liability Insurance** (CHF 2,000-5,000)
   - Minimum CHF 5 million coverage
   - Explicit coverage for electrical/thermal/battery work
   - Professional indemnity for on-site engineers
   - Confirmation deadline: 8 weeks before event

2. **Establish Independent Safety Officer** (CHF 2,500-5,000)
   - Licensed electrical engineer (Elektroinstallateur EFZ)
   - Authority to halt event, require modifications
   - Independent of organizers
   - Confirmation deadline: 4 weeks before event

3. **Mandatory Safety Training & Certification** (CHF 1,000-3,000 one-time)
   - 4-hour training (electrical, battery, thermal, emergency)
   - Certification exam (80% pass rate)
   - No participation without certification
   - Platform ready: 8 weeks before event

4. **Pre-Event Safety Inspection** (CHF 500-1,500)
   - Licensed electrician inspection
   - Safety certificate (Sicherheitsnachweis) issued
   - All issues resolved before event
   - Inspection: 2 weeks before event

5. **Real-Time Safety Monitoring** (CHF 11,200-25,800 one-time)
   - Sensor network (temperature, current, voltage, flow, leak)
   - AI anomaly detection
   - Alert system (visual, audio, SMS)
   - Automated safety triggers
   - System deployed: 1 week before event

**Total Critical Priority Cost:** CHF 17,200-40,300 (one-time development + per-event)

## Key Findings

### 1. Safety Governance Gap
**Finding:** No independent safety authority exists. Safety decisions are made by event organizers who have financial and reputational incentives to proceed.

**Impact:** High risk of safety concerns being minimized or overlooked.

**Solution:** Establish independent Safety Officer with authority to halt event.

### 2. Regulatory Compliance Gaps
**Finding:** Multiple regulatory compliance gaps identified (NIV, fire codes, insurance, environmental).

**Impact:** Risk of regulatory violations, event shutdown, liability exposure.

**Solution:** Complete compliance checklist, obtain all required permits and certificates.

### 3. Single Points of Failure
**Finding:** Critical safety systems have single points of failure (RCD, electrician, insurance, emergency stop).

**Impact:** System failure could result in catastrophic consequences.

**Solution:** Implement redundant safety systems, backup procedures.

### 4. Human Error Factors
**Finding:** Time pressure, fatigue, and mixed expertise levels not adequately addressed.

**Impact:** Increased probability of safety errors and incidents.

**Solution:** Mandatory safety training, continuous monitoring, daily briefings.

### 5. Pre-Event Validation Missing
**Finding:** No systematic pre-event safety validation (equipment inspection, safety case review, simulation).

**Impact:** Safety issues may not be identified until during event.

**Solution:** Pre-event inspection, safety simulations, equipment certification.
