## Safety Officer Role

### Authority and Independence

**Independence Requirement:**
- Safety Officer must be independent of event organizers
- Cannot be a Verein board member
- Cannot be an event organizer
- Cannot be a participant
- Reports directly to Verein board (not event organizers)

**Decision-Making Authority:**
- **Can halt event** if safety risk identified
- **Can halt specific activities** if safety risk identified
- **Can require modifications** to equipment or procedures
- **Can exclude participants** who violate safety protocols
- **Final authority** on safety decisions (can override organizers)
- **Cannot be overridden** by event organizers on safety matters

**Reporting Structure:**
- Reports to Verein board (written reports)
- Direct communication with event organizers (operational)
- Direct communication with participants (safety briefings)
- Direct communication with authorities (if required)

### Qualifications

**Required Qualifications:**
- Licensed electrical engineer (Elektroinstallateur EFZ)
- NIV installation license (Art. 15 or higher)
- Minimum 5 years experience with electrical installations
- Experience with battery systems or thermal systems (preferred)
- Safety certification (OSHA, NEBOSH, or equivalent preferred)

**Preferred Qualifications:**
- Experience with event safety
- Experience with maker spaces or hackathons
- Fire safety certification
- Medical first aid certification

### Responsibilities

#### Pre-Event Responsibilities

1. **Safety Plan Review:**
   - Review all safety plans and procedures
   - Verify compliance with regulations (NIV, fire codes)
   - Identify gaps and recommend improvements
   - Approve or reject safety plans

2. **Equipment Inspection:**
   - Inspect all equipment before event
   - Verify safety systems (RCD, fire suppression, containment)
   - Issue safety certificate (Sicherheitsnachweis) or reject
   - Document inspection results

3. **Safety Training:**
   - Review safety training content
   - Verify training completion for all participants
   - Conduct additional safety briefings if needed
   - Maintain training records

4. **Regulatory Compliance:**
   - Verify insurance coverage
   - Verify permits (fire safety, environmental)
   - Verify licensed electrician availability
   - Document compliance status

#### During-Event Responsibilities

1. **Continuous Monitoring:**
   - Continuous presence at event venue
   - Monitor safety systems (sensors, alerts)
   - Conduct daily safety inspections
   - Respond to safety incidents

2. **Safety Inspections:**
   - Daily safety inspections (morning, afternoon, evening)
   - Inspect all workstations and equipment
   - Verify safety system functionality
   - Document inspection results

3. **Incident Response:**
   - Respond to all safety incidents
   - Investigate incident causes
   - Implement corrective actions
   - Document incidents and responses

4. **Safety Briefings:**
   - Conduct daily safety briefings
   - Communicate safety updates
   - Answer participant safety questions
   - Reinforce safety protocols

#### Post-Event Responsibilities

1. **Incident Analysis:**
   - Analyze all incidents (root cause analysis)
   - Document lessons learned
   - Generate recommendations
   - Prepare incident report

2. **Safety Review:**
   - Review safety system effectiveness
   - Review participant compliance
   - Review regulatory compliance
   - Generate safety review report

3. **Recommendations:**
   - Provide recommendations for future events
   - Update safety procedures if needed
   - Update safety training if needed
   - Communicate recommendations to Verein board

### Compensation

**Daily Rate:** CHF 500-1,000 per day  
**Event Duration:** 3 days (plus 1 day pre-event, 1 day post-event)  
**Total:** CHF 2,500-5,000 per event

**Payment Terms:**
- 50% upon contract signing
- 50% upon completion of post-event report

## Decision-Making Protocols

### Safety Decision Authority

**Safety Officer Authority:**
- **Can halt event** - Immediate authority, cannot be overridden
- **Can halt specific activities** - Immediate authority, cannot be overridden
- **Can require modifications** - Immediate authority, cannot be overridden
- **Can exclude participants** - Immediate authority, cannot be overridden

**Event Organizer Authority:**
- **Cannot override** Safety Officer on safety matters
- **Can appeal** to Verein board (but event remains halted during appeal)
- **Must comply** with Safety Officer requirements

**Verein Board Authority:**
- **Final authority** on safety matters (can override Safety Officer)
- **Must consider** Safety Officer recommendations
- **Must document** any decision to override Safety Officer

### Decision-Making Process

#### Routine Safety Decisions
1. Safety Officer identifies safety concern
2. Safety Officer communicates concern to event organizers
3. Event organizers implement corrective action
4. Safety Officer verifies corrective action
5. Decision documented

#### Critical Safety Decisions (Event Halt)
1. Safety Officer identifies critical safety risk
2. Safety Officer immediately halts event or activity
3. Safety Officer communicates decision to event organizers and participants
4. Safety Officer documents decision and rationale
5. Event organizers implement corrective action
6. Safety Officer verifies corrective action
7. Safety Officer approves resumption of event
8. Decision documented

#### Appeal Process
1. Event organizers appeal Safety Officer decision to Verein board
2. Verein board reviews appeal (within 24 hours)
3. Verein board makes final decision
4. Decision documented
5. If appeal denied, event remains halted until Safety Officer approves

## Incident Reporting Protocol

### Incident Classification

**Level 1: Near Miss**
- Safety concern identified but no injury or damage
- Example: RCD trips, no injury
- Reporting: Safety Officer log, no external report

**Level 2: Minor Incident**
- Minor injury (first aid only) or minor damage
- Example: Small burn, minor electrical shock
- Reporting: Safety Officer log, incident report to event organizers

**Level 3: Serious Incident**
- Serious injury (medical attention required) or significant damage
- Example: Serious burn, electrical injury requiring hospital
- Reporting: Safety Officer log, incident report to event organizers and Verein board, regulatory report if required

**Level 4: Critical Incident**
- Life-threatening injury or catastrophic damage
- Example: Fire, explosion, serious electrocution
- Reporting: Safety Officer log, immediate notification to authorities, incident report to all stakeholders

### Incident Reporting Process

1. **Incident Occurs:**
   - Participant or safety personnel reports incident
   - Safety Officer responds immediately

2. **Immediate Response:**
   - Safety Officer assesses situation
   - Safety Officer implements immediate corrective action
   - Safety Officer secures area if needed

3. **Incident Documentation:**
   - Safety Officer documents incident (within 1 hour)
   - Incident report includes: time, location, description, cause, response, outcome

4. **Incident Analysis:**
   - Safety Officer conducts root cause analysis
   - Safety Officer identifies contributing factors
   - Safety Officer generates recommendations

5. **Incident Communication:**
   - Safety Officer communicates incident to appropriate stakeholders
   - Safety Officer provides incident report
   - Safety Officer implements recommendations

6. **Post-Incident Review:**
   - Safety Officer reviews incident with Safety Committee
   - Safety Officer updates safety procedures if needed
   - Safety Officer documents lessons learned

## Emergency Response Procedures

### Emergency Types

1. **Fire Emergency:**
   - Fire detected (smoke, heat, flame)
   - Fire suppression activation
   - Evacuation if needed
   - Fire department notification

2. **Medical Emergency:**
   - Participant injury or illness
   - First aid response
   - Hospital transport if needed
   - Emergency contact notification

3. **Electrical Emergency:**
   - Electrical fault or shock
   - System shutdown
   - Medical response if injury
   - Electrician response

4. **Battery Emergency:**
   - Battery thermal runaway
   - Fire risk
   - Evacuation if needed
   - Emergency response

5. **Environmental Emergency:**
   - Oil leak or spill
   - Environmental contamination
   - Containment and cleanup
   - Regulatory notification if required

### Emergency Response Process

1. **Emergency Detected:**
   - Sensor alert or human observation
   - Safety Officer notified immediately

2. **Immediate Response:**
   - Safety Officer assesses situation
   - Safety Officer implements emergency procedures
   - Safety Officer coordinates response

3. **Emergency Communication:**
   - Safety Officer notifies all safety personnel
   - Safety Officer notifies event organizers
   - Safety Officer notifies participants (if evacuation needed)
   - Safety Officer notifies authorities (if required)

4. **Emergency Resolution:**
   - Safety Officer coordinates resolution
   - Safety Officer verifies safety before resumption
   - Safety Officer documents emergency

5. **Post-Emergency Review:**
   - Safety Officer conducts post-emergency review
   - Safety Officer identifies improvements
   - Safety Officer updates procedures

   - Post-event review completed

3. **Safety Personnel:**
   - All required personnel recruited
   - Continuous presence during event
   - All responsibilities fulfilled
   - Incident response effective

4. **Decision-Making:**

   - All decisions documented
   - Appeal process functional

5. **Communication:**
   - All communication protocols followed

   - Incident reports timely and complete
